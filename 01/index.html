Try this:

  • For each face, decide what that person is feeling. Say it to yourself, or tell a friend.
  • Click a button to put the face in context. Try out some different contexts; what do you think the person is feeling now?

Human faces are very expressive. A multitude of muscles control facial movements that show how you feel. But a facial expression doesn’t always equal emotion - for example, you can frown from anger, but also from focused concentration.

We percieve emotion through facial expression, context and more! To accurately read someone else’s emotion, you have to look at what they’re doing, who they’re with, and what their whole body is saying.
